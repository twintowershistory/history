Twin Towers History

1973

The Twin Towers of the original World Trade Center were officially dedicated on April 4, 1973. The towers, designed by Minoru Yamasaki and Emery Roth & Sons, were the tallest buildings in the world at the time, standing 1,368 and 1,362 feet tall, respectively.

1974

The South Tower (2 WTC) was completed in late 1974, one year after the North Tower (1 WTC). The twin towers were connected at the 11th and 12th floors by a sky lobby. At the time of their completion, the towers were the tallest and largest buildings in the world.

1975

In 1975, the South Tower was the first building in the world to surpass the roofline of the Empire State Building. The two towers became icons of the city of New York and were featured prominently in movies, television shows, and other media.

1976

The two towers became home to numerous businesses, including the world’s biggest brokerage firm, the world’s largest commodities exchange, and a number of restaurants and stores.

1977

In 1977, the World Trade Center hosted a fireworks show to celebrate the United States Bicentennial. The show attracted a crowd of more than 250,000 people, many of whom watched from the observation deck of the South Tower.

1978

In 1978, the World Trade Center was the site of the first International Trade Center Conference, which was attended by business leaders from around the world.

1979

In 1979, the Twin Towers became home to the Windows on the World restaurant, a fine dining establishment located on the 107th floor of the North Tower.

1980 and '81

The Twin Towers continued to be a major tourist attraction in the 1980s, and in 1981 the observation deck of the South Tower was renovated and reopened to the public.

1982

In 1982, the observation deck of the North Tower was also renovated and reopened to the public. The Twin Towers were often referred to as “the most popular tourist attraction in New York City.”

1983

In 1983, the World Trade Center hosted a summit of world leaders, including U.S. President Ronald Reagan, French President François Mitterrand, and British Prime Minister Margaret Thatcher.

1984

In 1984, the Twin Towers became the site of the world’s first public satellite broadcast. The broadcast, which was watched by millions of people around the world, featured a live performance by legendary singer Paul Simon.

1985

In 1985, the Twin Towers were the site of the first international AIDS conference. The conference was attended by thousands of people from around the world and was the first major event to focus on the AIDS epidemic.

1986

In 1986, the Twin Towers were the site of a historic summit between U.S. President Ronald Reagan and Soviet leader Mikhail Gorbachev. The summit was seen as a major step toward ending the Cold War between the two countries.

1987

In 1987, the Twin Towers became the world’s tallest buildings again when the Sears Tower in Chicago was completed. The Twin Towers remained the tallest buildings in the world until the completion of the Petronas Towers in Malaysia in 1998.

1988

In 1988, the Twin Towers were the site of a major international trade fair. The fair, which was attended by leaders from around the world, was the largest such event ever held in the United States.

1989

In 1989, The Twin Towers were a professional wrestling tag team in the World Wrestling Federation (WWF) from 1989 to 1990, consisting of Akeem and the Big Boss Man. They are best known for a main event feud with the Mega Powers.

1990

In 1990, the Twin Towers were the site of a summit between U.S. President George H. W. Bush and Japanese Prime Minister Toshiki Kaifu. The summit was seen as a major step toward improving relations between the two countries.
